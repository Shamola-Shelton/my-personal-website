About Me

Hello there! My name is Shelton Shamola, and I was born on December 28th, 1998. I'm a proud Kenyan, a passionate learner, and someone who thrives on creativity, logic, and curiosity. I hold a Bachelor's Degree in Business Information Technology, and my journey has been one of discovery, growth, and a pursuit of purpose.

I’m a mix of introvert and extrovert — I cherish quiet moments indoors coding for hours, but I also enjoy occasional adventures, laughter with friends, and creating memories with those I love. I'm deeply grounded in my Christian faith, which serves as my moral compass and gives direction to my life and goals.

Music is a huge part of my identity. It’s my sanctuary. My playlists are dominated by lyrical genius — J. Cole, Drake, Kendrick Lamar, and the soulful melodies of Rihanna and James Arthur. These artists have shaped my moods, sparked my ideas, and helped me feel understood.

I also bleed red — not just in biology, but in football. Manchester United is my team, and I’ve supported them through thick and thin. Football reminds me of life — it's unpredictable, emotional, and full of passion.

But above all, I love coding. There’s something magical about turning a blank screen into something functional and beautiful. Programming gives me the ability to solve problems, build useful things, and express creativity in a logical way. I'm especially interested in web development — bringing ideas to life on the internet, one line of code at a time.

I spend most of my days in front of a computer, either coding, reading documentation, watching tutorials, or debugging. But life isn’t all semicolons and divs — I have a soft side. I’m currently in a relationship with an amazing woman named Graca Tae. She’s supportive, kind, and brings joy into my world. We share laughs, dreams, and a vision of growing together.

My ambition is to become a world-class software developer. I believe in continuous learning, consistency, and building meaningful projects. This website is just the beginning of that journey. If you’re here to learn about me, collaborate, or just browse — welcome, and thank you.
